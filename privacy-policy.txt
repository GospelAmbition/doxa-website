Privacy Policy

Last Updated: 13th January 2026

1. Introduction

Doxa Foundation ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our websites and use our services. This policy applies to all users of our websites, including both public visitors and registered users.

By using our websites, you consent to the data practices described in this policy. If you do not agree with the practices described in this policy, please do not use our websites.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Public Website (Content Viewing)

When you visit our public website to view content, we do not use cookies or tracking technologies. However, we may collect the following information when you voluntarily submit forms:

Contact Form:
- Name
- Email address
- Message content
- Cloudflare Turnstile verification data (for spam prevention)

Adoption Form:
- Church name
- WAGF Block selection
- Prayer Champion details:
  - First name
  - Last name
  - Email address
  - Phone number
  - Role/position
- Adoption commitment confirmation

2.2 Logged-In User Experience

When you create an account and sign up for email reminders, we collect and store:
- Email address
- Reminder preferences (including times you choose to receive reminders)
- Account preferences and settings
- Any other information you voluntarily provide

2.3 Automatically Collected Information

We may automatically collect certain information about your device and how you interact with our websites, including:
- IP address
- Browser type and version
- Operating system
- Pages visited and time spent on pages
- Referring website addresses
- Date and time of access

Note: We currently do not use analytics services, but we reserve the right to implement analytics tools in the future. If we do, we will update this Privacy Policy accordingly.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

3.1 Contact Form Submissions
- To respond to your inquiries and messages
- To provide customer support
- To communicate with you about your requests

3.2 Adoption Form Submissions
- To process and confirm your church's adoption of a people group
- To connect with your designated Prayer Champion
- To send prayer updates and resources related to the adopted people group
- To manage our adoption program

3.3 Logged-In User Accounts
- To create and manage your account
- To send email reminders at the times you have selected
- To personalize your experience
- To communicate with you about your account and our services

3.4 General Uses
- To improve our websites and services
- To ensure security and prevent fraud
- To comply with legal obligations
- To enforce our terms and conditions

4. Third-Party Services

4.1 Cloudflare Turnstile

We use Cloudflare Turnstile, a service provided by Cloudflare, Inc., to protect our contact form from spam and abuse. When you submit the contact form, Cloudflare Turnstile may collect certain information about your device and browser to verify that you are a human user. This service may set cookies or use other tracking technologies. For more information about Cloudflare's privacy practices, please visit: https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/

4.2 Rocketmail

We use Rocketmail to send email reminders to registered users. When you sign up for email reminders, your email address and reminder preferences are shared with Rocketmail for the purpose of delivering emails at your chosen times. For information about Rocketmail's privacy practices, please refer to their privacy policy.

4.3 External CRM

Form submissions (contact form and adoption form) are stored in an external Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system. This system is used solely for managing and organizing the information you provide to us. We do not share this information with third parties for marketing or other purposes.

4.4 Future Analytics

We may implement analytics services in the future to better understand how visitors use our websites. If we do, we will update this Privacy Policy to disclose the specific analytics provider and how data is collected and used.

5. Data Storage and Retention

5.1 Storage Location

Your information is stored in:
- Our external CRM system (for form submissions)
- Our website database (for logged-in user accounts)
- Rocketmail's systems (for email delivery)

5.2 Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. Specifically:
- Form submissions are retained until you request deletion
- Logged-in user account information is retained for as long as your account is active
- We will delete your information upon your request, subject to any legal obligations to retain certain data

6. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. We may share your information only in the following circumstances:

6.1 Service Providers

We may share information with third-party service providers who perform services on our behalf, such as:
- Rocketmail (for email delivery)
- Cloudflare (for spam protection)
- Our CRM provider (for data storage and management)

These service providers are contractually obligated to protect your information and use it only for the purposes we specify.

6.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities (e.g., a court or government agency).

6.3 Business Transfers

In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such change in ownership or control of your personal information.

6.4 With Your Consent

We may share your information with your explicit consent or at your direction.

7. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information:

7.1 Access and Portability

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you and to receive a copy of that information in a portable format.

7.2 Correction

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information.

7.3 Deletion

You have the right to request deletion of your personal information. We will honor such requests, subject to any legal obligations to retain certain information.

7.4 Opt-Out

For logged-in users, you can:
- Update your email reminder preferences at any time
- Unsubscribe from email reminders
- Delete your account

7.5 Objection to Processing

You have the right to object to our processing of your personal information in certain circumstances.

7.6 Restriction of Processing

You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal information in certain circumstances.

7.7 Withdraw Consent

Where we rely on your consent to process your information, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided in Section 11 (Contact Us).

8. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Children's Privacy

Our websites are not intended for children under the age of 13 (or the applicable age of consent in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe we have collected information from a child, please contact us immediately, and we will take steps to delete that information.

10. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have data protection laws that differ from those in your country. By using our websites, you consent to the transfer of your information to these countries. We take appropriate measures to ensure that your information receives an adequate level of protection in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any material changes by:
- Posting the new Privacy Policy on this page
- Updating the "Last Updated" date
- Sending you an email notification (for registered users)

Your continued use of our websites after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.

12. Contact Us

If you have any questions, concerns, or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please contact us: https://doxa.life/contact-us

For users in the European Union, you also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority if you believe we have not addressed your concerns adequately.

13. Additional Information for Specific Jurisdictions

13.1 European Union (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Union, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including:
- The right to be informed about data collection and use
- The right of access to your personal data
- The right to rectification of inaccurate data
- The right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- The right to restrict processing
- The right to data portability
- The right to object to processing
- Rights related to automated decision-making and profiling

Our legal basis for processing your personal information includes:
- Your consent (for email reminders and form submissions)
- Performance of a contract (for managing adoptions)
- Legitimate interests (for website security and improvement)
- Compliance with legal obligations

13.2 California (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), including:
- The right to know what personal information is collected, used, shared, or sold
- The right to delete personal information
- The right to opt-out of the sale of personal information (we do not sell personal information)
- The right to non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights
- The right to correct inaccurate personal information
- The right to limit the use of sensitive personal information

13.3 Other Jurisdictions

We strive to comply with applicable privacy laws in all jurisdictions where we operate. If you have questions about your rights under your local privacy laws, please contact us.
